Underground Garbage Disposal

A design and prototype for New York City's cleanliness.

Agenda

Garbage overflow in NYC streets causes sidewalks to become dirty and difficult to walk through! On top of the irritating idor that stems from the piled up trash because of littering and a weak drainage system.

The Underground Garbage disposal system can assit this City crises by

  • Storing garbage underground, giving a bigger space for deposits.
  • Allowing for a rapid and seamingless collection of trash and reducing the amount of trash bags used.
  • By moving the trash underground, there would be less visibility of garbage in public areas.
  • The design of the underground system allows the pungent odor to be kept bellow street level and, reduces pollutants that are released into the air!

Objective

  • Design a garbage can which it's top piece aligns with the dimensions of the garbage truck's rear.
  • The design is an underground trash can that can easily be emptied into a garbage truck. This would make trash collection more efficient and effective, and trash piles would be eliminated.

Main Issues

  • New York City produces approximately 44 million pounds (about 20 million kilograms) of waste daily, with about 24 million pounds collected by the city's sanitation department. with New York annually producing approximately 2.5 million tons of trash per year. This figure has been consistent, with an average of nearly 2.6 million tons since 2020. The waste generation varies across boroughs, with Brooklyn producing the most trash on average. or about 5,511,556,555 Pounds of trash yearly.
  • The drainage system in city is vulnerable to rainstorm-- It often floods and does not drain fully

Solutions

Set up organized trash bins, possibly larger sizes. Pile the trash underground so it could occupy more space Implement green infrastructure. However, some European countries like the Netherlands already has a similar system implemented, but the need for this system is a neccessary for the important city NYC is within the United States. Additionally, it can be helpful to report any cloggeds sewers, and upgrading the combined sewer systems. Ultimatley, NYC can start building these underground garbage systems in new developed and rezoned areas in all boroughs, and gradually roll out this system over the years!
